HTML Quotation and Citation Elements

1. The {blockquote} for Quotations.
It defines a quote that is extracted from an external source and therefore indents it.
It is a good practice to include the link to the website of which the quote belongs to. This is done by including it in the "blockquote" element in the format;
{ cite="The link to the website."}
An example could be a quote from the WWF's website:

For 60 years, WWF has worked to help people and nature thrive. As the world's leading conservation organization, WWF works in nearly 100 countries. At every level, we collaborate with people around the world to develop and deliver innovative solutions that protect communities, wildlife, and the places in which they live.
2. The {abbr} tag for Abbreviations
This tag defines an abbreviations of which when marked, can give useful information to browsers, translation systems and search-engines.
When you mouse over the abbreviated word, it will display its full version.
It's format is {abbr title="Full meaning" the abbreviate} closed inside angle brackets.
Examples would be:
The WHO was founded in 1948.
